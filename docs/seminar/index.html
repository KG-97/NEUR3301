MDMA and the Brain:
signal, not settlement.

MDMA produces a rapid, serotonin-dominant monoamine state that may reduce defensive responding and support trauma-focused learning. In mice, it can reopen a social-reward critical period; whether that mechanism occurs in humans or explains PTSD outcomes remains unproven. Clinical trials show a strong package-level signal, but unblinding, safety reporting, durability and causal separation remain unresolved.

Evidence boundaries

Confidence is claim-specific. A strong package-level clinical signal does not prove a drug-only mechanism.

High confidenceMDMA is a transporter substrate promoting monoamine efflux; it increases oxytocin, cortisol and prolactin in controlled human studies; acute hyperthermia and hyponatraemia can be fatal.
Moderate confidenceMDMA may facilitate fear learning and reconsolidation. Mouse critical-period reopening is robust; human mediation remains untested.
Unknown / unprovenThat a few screened clinical sessions cause lasting human serotonergic neurotoxicity, or that the mouse social-reward critical period explains PTSD outcomes.
