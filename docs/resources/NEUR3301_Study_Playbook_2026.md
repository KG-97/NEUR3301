# NEUR3301 — Marks-First Study Playbook

## The assessment reality

- Seminar: 30% total. The video is 25%; peer questions and Q&A participation form the remaining 5%.
- Test 1: 10% MCQ on lectures 1–7 (development and differentiation), 24 August 2026.
- Test 2: 10% MCQ on lectures 8–13 (synaptic physiology and plasticity), 7 September 2026.
- Final: 50%. Two hours, 8–10 long-answer questions, choose four. That is approximately 30 minutes per answer.

Recognition is not readiness. Readiness means producing an accurate mechanism without prompts, interpreting an intervention, and naming what the evidence cannot establish.

## Weekly operating system

### Lecture days — Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday

Within 12 hours of each lecture:

1. **Compress (10 min):** write the lecture's causal chain in no more than six arrows.
2. **Retrieve (15 min):** close everything and redraw/explain it from memory.
3. **Interrogate (15 min):** answer one blocker/knockout/lesion question and predict the result before checking notes.
4. **Encode (10 min):** make only the flashcards you got wrong or could not explain.

### Thursday — integration

- Link the week's lectures with two bridge questions.
- Example: How do trophic competition, apoptosis and activity-dependent pruning solve different parts of the wiring problem?
- Draw one mechanism figure from memory.

### Friday — test pressure

- Complete 10 closed-book MCQs in Exam Forge.
- Log each miss as one of: knowledge gap, mechanism reversal, species/context overclaim, or question-reading error.
- Retest only the misses the following day.

### Saturday — final-exam conversion

- From week 3 onward, complete one 30-minute long answer.
- Use 3 minutes to plan, 24 to write, and 3 to repair.
- Minimum viable top-band answer: thesis, causal mechanism, one experiment, one limitation, direct conclusion.

### Sunday — seminar + recovery

- Spend 60–90 minutes on the MDMA seminar until the evidence deck is stable.
- Then stop. Consolidation is part of learning; exhausted rereading is mostly theatre.

## Immediate move — week 1

1. Glia I: distinguish astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, microglia and ependymal cells by mechanism, not adjectives.
2. Growth/trophic factors: master competition for target-derived survival signals and separate survival from axon guidance.
3. Seminar: lock the thesis and allocate speakers A/B/C using the timed deck. Do not begin by making pretty slides.
4. Create an error log with four columns: question, my answer, failure type, corrected mechanism.

## Non-negotiable answer grammar

Use this chain whenever possible:

**Manipulation → molecular/cellular change → circuit consequence → measured outcome → alternative interpretation.**

Example: block TrkA → reduced NGF survival signalling → increased intrinsic apoptosis in NGF-dependent neurons → fewer surviving neurons/afferents → but confirm the phenotype is not caused by altered target tissue or axon access.

## Seminar thesis

**MDMA produces a rapid, serotonin-dominant monoamine state that may reduce defensive responding and support trauma-focused learning. In mice, it can reopen a social-reward critical period; whether that mechanism occurs in humans or explains PTSD outcomes remains unproven. Clinical trials show a strong package-level signal, but unblinding, safety reporting, durability and causal separation remain unresolved.**

This thesis gives the talk an argument. “Here are some facts about ecstasy” does not.
